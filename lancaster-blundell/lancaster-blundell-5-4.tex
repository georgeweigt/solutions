(5.4)
The Lagrangian for a free particle is $L=-mc^2/\gamma$.
Find an expression for $L$, $p$ and $H$ when $v\ll c$.

\bigskip
\hrule

\bigskip
We have
\begin{align*}
p&=\gamma mv
\tag{5.23}
\\
H&=\gamma mc^2
\tag{5.24}
\end{align*}

For $v\ll c$, $\gamma$ is near unity.
\begin{equation*}
\gamma=\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-v^2/c^2}}\approx1
\end{equation*}

Then for $\gamma=1$ we have
\begin{align*}
L&=-mc^2
\\
p&=mv
\\
H&=mc^2
\end{align*}
