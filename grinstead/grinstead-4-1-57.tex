\section*{57.}
Prove that $A$ neither attracts nor repels $B$ if and only if $A$ and $B$
are independent.
\bigskip
\noindent
First the implication.
Let $A$ and $B$ be independent.
Then
$$P(A\cap B)=P(A)P(B)$$
Hence
\begin{eqnarray*}
{P(A\cap B)\over P(A)}&=&P(B)\\
{P(A\cap B)\over P(B)}&=&P(A)
\end{eqnarray*}
Now prove the converse.
